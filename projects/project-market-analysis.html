Market Analysis for a Fintech Startup

How I identified a 3× larger opportunity through TAM/SAM/SOM modelling, customer segmentation, and 120-person primary research.

Overview

A seed-stage fintech startup needed to validate their market opportunity before approaching investors. The goal was to size the addressable market, identify the most attractive customer segments, and uncover white-space opportunities competitors had missed.

The project ran over six weeks and combined primary research (interviews and surveys) with secondary research (industry reports, competitor data, regulatory filings).

Methodology

I used a structured top-down and bottom-up approach to market sizing, anchored by three frameworks:

  • TAM / SAM / SOM modelling using industry reports and census data
  • 15 in-depth customer discovery interviews across three target segments
  • Competitive benchmarking against 6 direct and 4 indirect competitors
  • Survey of 120 potential users to validate willingness-to-pay and feature priorities

Key Findings

The original hypothesis focused on consumer personal finance. Through the research, I discovered that the SMB (small business) payments segment was 3× larger, significantly less crowded, and had a much higher willingness-to-pay.

  • SMB segment TAM: $4.2B vs. consumer TAM of $1.4B in the target geography
  • Three distinct SMB sub-segments ranked by acquisition cost and LTV potential
  • Competitor gap: no player offered real-time multi-currency reconciliation — the #1 pain point
  • 3-year revenue forecast built under three scenarios (conservative, base, optimistic)

Outcome

The startup pivoted their product roadmap to target the SMB segment. The analysis and pitch deck were used in their seed funding round. I subsequently supported two follow-up workshops to refine the go-to-market strategy.
